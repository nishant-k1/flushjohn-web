# USPS Form 1583 - Identity Verification Guide

## üìã **What is Form 1583?**

**USPS Form 1583** (Application for Delivery of Mail Through Agent) is required by the United States Postal Service (USPS) to authorize your virtual address service (mail center) to receive mail on your behalf.

**Why it's needed:**

- USPS requires identity verification for mail forwarding/receiving services
- Your virtual address service needs this to legally receive your mail
- Required for receiving GBP verification postcard
- Required for all mail forwarding services

---

## ‚úÖ **Step-by-Step: Complete Form 1583**

### **Step 1: Download the Form**

1. **Get Form 1583:**

   - Download from USPS website: https://www.usps.com/forms/_pdf/ps1583.pdf
   - Or your virtual address service may provide it
   - Or they may have an online form

2. **Check with your service:**
   - Many services have online Form 1583 submission
   - Log into your virtual address account
   - Look for "Complete Form 1583" or "Identity Verification"

---

### **Step 2: Fill Out the Form**

#### **Section 1: Mail Recipient Information**

**Fill in your details:**

1. **Name:**

   - Your full legal name (as it appears on ID)
   - Or business name if receiving business mail

2. **Address:**

   - Your current address (India address is fine)
   - This is where you currently receive mail

3. **Phone Number:**

   - Your contact number
   - Can be India number

4. **Email:**
   - Your email address

#### **Section 2: Agent Information (Your Virtual Address Service)**

**Fill in your virtual address service details:**

1. **Agent Name:**

   - Name of your virtual address service
   - Example: "iPostal1" or "Anytime Mailbox"

2. **Agent Address:**

   - The virtual address they provided
   - Example: "123 Main St, Suite 456, Dover, DE 19901"

3. **Agent Contact:**
   - Service's contact information
   - Usually provided by the service

#### **Section 3: Mail to be Forwarded**

**Check the boxes for mail types you want to receive:**

- ‚úÖ First-Class Mail
- ‚úÖ Priority Mail
- ‚úÖ Packages
- ‚úÖ Certified Mail (if needed)
- ‚úÖ Registered Mail (if needed)

**For GBP verification, you need:**

- ‚úÖ First-Class Mail (postcards are first-class)

#### **Section 4: Signature and Notarization**

**IMPORTANT:** Form 1583 must be **notarized**!

1. **Sign the form:**

   - Sign with your legal signature
   - Date the form

2. **Get it notarized:**
   - Find a notary public
   - They will witness your signature
   - They will stamp/seal the form

---

### **Step 3: Get Notarized**

#### **Option 1: Notary in India**

**Find a notary in India:**

1. **Search for notaries:**

   - Google: "notary public near me [your city]"
   - Check with local banks (many have notaries)
   - Check with law firms
   - Check with government offices

2. **What to bring:**

   - Completed Form 1583
   - Valid government-issued ID (passport, driver's license)
   - Proof of address (if required)

3. **Cost:**

   - Usually ‚Çπ50-500 in India
   - Varies by location

4. **Process:**
   - Show ID to notary
   - Sign form in front of notary
   - Notary stamps/seals the form
   - Get notarized copy

#### **Option 2: Online Notary (If Available)**

**Some services offer online notarization:**

1. **Check if your virtual address service offers:**

   - Some services have online notary options
   - May cost extra but more convenient

2. **Online notary services:**
   - Notarize.com
   - DocuSign Notary
   - Check if available in India

#### **Option 3: US Embassy/Consulate**

**If in major Indian city:**

1. **US Embassy/Consulate:**
   - May offer notary services
   - Check their website for availability
   - Usually requires appointment
   - May charge fee

---

### **Step 4: Submit to Your Virtual Address Service**

**After notarization:**

1. **Scan the notarized form:**

   - Create high-quality PDF or image
   - Make sure notary stamp is clear
   - Include all pages

2. **Submit to service:**

   - Upload through their website/portal
   - Or email to their support
   - Follow their specific instructions

3. **Also submit ID:**
   - Copy of your government-issued ID
   - **Passport is best** (has English, universally accepted)
   - **Aadhar Card** - May work, but contact service first (see `AADHAR_CARD_FORM_1583.md`)
   - Driver's license works too
   - Make sure it's clear and readable
   - **If using Aadhar:** Contact service support first to confirm acceptance

---

### **Step 5: Wait for Approval**

**Processing time:**

- Usually 1-3 business days
- Service will verify your identity
- They'll notify you when approved
- Once approved, they can receive your mail

## ‚ö†Ô∏è **Common Issues & Solutions**

### **Issue: Can't find notary in India**

**Solutions:**

- Check with local banks (many have notaries)
- Check with law firms
- Check with government offices
- Use online notary if available
- Check US Embassy/Consulate

### **Issue: Notary doesn't understand English form**

**Solutions:**

- Explain it's for US mail forwarding
- Show them the form (it's mostly self-explanatory)
- They just need to witness your signature
- Most notaries can handle it

### **Issue: Service rejects the form**

**Solutions:**

- Check if notary stamp is clear
- Ensure all sections are filled
- Verify ID copy is clear
- Contact service support for specific requirements
- Resubmit with corrections

### **Issue: Service doesn't accept Aadhar Card**

**Solutions:**

1. **Contact service support:**

   - Explain Aadhar is valid government ID in India
   - Ask if they can accept it
   - Provide Aadhar card details

2. **Provide additional documentation:**

   - Aadhar card + Passport (if available)
   - Aadhar card + Driver's License (if available)
   - Aadhar card + Proof of address
   - English translation of Aadhar (if needed)

3. **Alternative: Get Passport**

   - If you don't have passport, consider getting one
   - Passport is universally accepted
   - Makes process easier

4. **Check service's ID requirements:**
   - Some services list accepted IDs
   - May accept Aadhar with explanation
   - May need to provide business registration documents

### **Issue: Takes too long to approve**

**Solutions:**

- Usually 1-3 business days
- Contact service if longer
- Check spam folder for emails
- Verify all documents submitted correctly
